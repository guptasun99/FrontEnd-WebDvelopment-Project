üìö Financial Knowledge Center

üìà Compound Interest - The 8th Wonder

Albert Einstein allegedly called compound interest "the eighth wonder of the world." It's the interest calculated on both the initial principal and the accumulated interest from previous periods. Starting early allows your investments to grow exponentially over time.

üíµ Simple vs Compound Interest

Simple interest is calculated only on the principal amount, making it straightforward but less profitable. Compound interest earns interest on interest, making it more powerful for long-term wealth building.

üìä Understanding CAGR

CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) is the mean annual growth rate of an investment over a specified period. It's useful for comparing the growth rates of different investments or portfolios.

üéØ The Power of Starting Early

A 25-year-old investing $200/month at 7% return will have ~$525,000 by age 65. A 35-year-old doing the same will have only ~$244,000. Starting 10 years earlier more than doubles your wealth!

üìä Diversification

Don't put all your eggs in one basket. Spread investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) to reduce risk. A well-diversified portfolio can weather market volatility better.

üíº Index Funds vs Active Funds

Index funds track market indices with low fees and often outperform actively managed funds over time. Warren Buffett recommends S&P 500 index funds for most investors.

üè¶ Emergency Fund First

Before investing, build an emergency fund covering 3-6 months of expenses. This prevents you from selling investments at a loss during emergencies.

üìà Dollar-Cost Averaging

Invest a fixed amount regularly regardless of market conditions. This strategy reduces the impact of volatility and removes emotion from investing decisions.

APR (Annual Percentage Rate)

The annual rate charged for borrowing, expressed as a percentage that represents the actual yearly cost over the term of a loan.

APY (Annual Percentage Yield)

The real rate of return earned on an investment, taking into account the effect of compounding interest.

Principal

The original amount of money borrowed or invested, before any interest or returns are added.

Amortization

The process of paying off debt with regular payments over time, where each payment covers interest and reduces principal.

ROI (Return on Investment)

A measure of the profitability of an investment, calculated as (Gain - Cost) / Cost √ó 100.

Equity

The value of ownership in an asset after subtracting liabilities. In real estate, it's home value minus mortgage balance.

üí∞ 50/30/20 Rule

Allocate your after-tax income: 50% for needs (rent, utilities), 30% for wants (entertainment, dining), and 20% for savings and debt repayment.

üéØ Pay Yourself First

Automate your savings by setting up automatic transfers to savings/investment accounts right after payday. Treat savings as a non-negotiable expense.

üí≥ High-Interest Debt First

Pay off high-interest debt (credit cards) before low-interest debt (student loans). Consider the debt avalanche method for maximum savings.

üè† House Affordability

The 28/36 rule suggests spending no more than 28% of gross income on housing and no more than 36% on total debt payments.
